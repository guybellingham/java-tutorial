java.awt.BorderLayout

 GridLayout

The BorderLayout divides a container into at most five cells (not all the cells need contain a component). Often it is used as the 'top level' layout for an application with a menu or toolbar in the north cell, the main work area in the center and a status bar along the bottom in the 'south' cell.

Only a single Container or Component can be placed at each point of the compass, with a statements like the following :

setLayout(new BorderLayout());
add("North", button1);
add("South", checkBox1); 

Note: This is the default LayoutManager for Containers, Window, Dialog, & Frame.

This LayoutManager does not always respect the preferred size of a Component, depending upon their placement, components will be 'stretched to fit'.

Components added to the "North" or "South" retain only their preferred height, those added to either the "East " or "West" regions, maintain their preferred width but are stretched to fit between the north and south regions.
The Component at the "Center" fills whatever area is left over.
